# Gameplay Loop

## Loop Summary

Orbitfall follows a phased pressure loop:

1. Consume finite reserves while restoring critical functionality and building extensions.
2. Stabilise the damaged ship or ship-like habitat long enough to reach descent capability.
3. Decide descent timing using hard capability gate plus soft readiness layer.
4. Establish a hostile beachhead while the ship remains fallback, staging, and support.
5. Scale sustained hostile-world industry under persistent environmental pressure.

## Early Loop: Damaged Ship or Ship-Like Habitat Survival

- Opening resources are finite:
  - Finite oxygen reserve
  - Finite water reserve
  - Ration-based food
  - Limited power access
- Broken infrastructure is part of the start state, including repair and reconnect work on critical routes.
- Player does not begin with a stable sustainable life-support system.
- The player is initially consuming reserves rather than operating sustainable production.
- The first stabilisation burst should be quick and legible.
- Multiple viable follow-up actions should exist immediately after this point.
- After first stabilisation, priorities should branch.

Primary activities:

- Urgent repair and reconnect tasks on critical routes.
- Basic power upkeep to keep repairs and life support actions possible.
- Research and build choices that extend survival without granting permanence.

Representative pattern, not a locked implementation:

- Restore oxygen distribution before generation.
- Add generation later, consuming scarce resources and creating new pressure.
- Other systems should follow similar coupling where solving one introduces new pressure elsewhere.

Primary output: enough stability and runway to make descent preparation possible.

## Transition Loop: Readiness Judgment

- Hard capability gate enables descent at all.
- Soft readiness layer determines whether post-descent survival is realistic.
- Player chooses descent timing under growing pressure, not under a single hard timer alone.
- The player should feel increasing risk in delaying descent, not just increasing comfort.
- The judgment point should remain meaningful even after descent becomes technically possible.

Primary output: player-owned go or no-go descent decision with consequences.

## Mid Loop: Hostile Beachhead

- Descent leads to a harsh beachhead phase, not immediate colony normalization.
- Ship remains relevant as fallback, staging, and support at first.
- Ship dependence should reduce gradually as planetary systems take over.
- Foothold expansion should stay careful and pressured.
- Player must build survivability while managing ongoing hostile conditions.

Primary output: durable foothold that can support sustained hostile-world operations.

## Late Loop: Industrial Mastery Under Persistent Hostility

- The planet never fully becomes safe.
- Environmental hostility persists and is engineered around, not erased.
- Endgame target is sustained hostile-world industrial operation.
- Loop should not normalize into comfort-state colony flow.
- Scaling systems should remain under pressure rather than reaching a fully solved steady state.

Primary output: stable, high-value production under continuous world pressure.

## Replayability Levers

- Short intro structure with variable details rather than long fixed scripting.
- Optional sealed hazard room with meaningful reward-risk trade.
- Blocked sections and access-order differences.
- Variation in repair topology and resource access patterns.

## Loop Identity

- Early game is triage and time-buying.
- Mid game is beachhead survival.
- Late game is mastery under persistent hostility.
